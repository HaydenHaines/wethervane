# Decisions Log

Key architectural and methodological decisions for the US Political Covariation Model. Each decision records the date, what was decided, why, and a link to a formal ADR where applicable.

For full context on any decision, see the linked ADR in `docs/adr/`.

| Date | Decision | Rationale | ADR |
|------|----------|-----------|-----|
| 2026-03-18 | Primary goal is community identity discovery; vote prediction is a validation mechanism, not the product. | Distinguishing preference from mobilization is where academic voter models consistently fail. Building a model optimized for prediction risks Goodharting the metric — we'd optimize for election accuracy and lose the structural insight that motivated the project. | -- |
| 2026-03-18 | K=7 selected for NMF community types (revised after empirical comparison of K=4,6,7,8,9,11). R² is non-monotonic in K — different NMF local optima per K. K=7 chosen for geographic analysis: correctly separates Asian from Knowledge Worker/WFH (different geographic logics — Atlanta suburbs vs. university towns must not be fused). K=6 has better R² (0.681) but fuses these two types. Generic baseline (~60% of tracts) is a valid finding — most Deep South suburban tracts are genuinely heterogeneous. Visualization strategy: map membership intensity per community (continuous scale), not dominant type. Membership entropy 1.234/1.946 (63%). See `docs/references/methods/nmf-community-detection.md`. | -- |
| 2026-03-18 | Stage 3 initial validation: NNLS R²=0.661 (K=7, Strong). Non-political NMF community structure explains 66% of 2020 presidential vote-share variance (FL+GA+AL, 9,295 tracts, vote-weighted). Core hypothesis confirmed at strong level. Individual NNLS θ unreliable for sparse components (c2=240%, c3=156%); direct weighted means used for profiles. Stage 3 Bayesian model will regularize via priors. | -- |
| 2026-03-18 | Stage 3 multi-election validation: R²=0.689 (2016 pres), 0.636 (2018 gov, FL+GA only), 0.661 (2020 pres). Core hypothesis confirmed across three independent election cycles — not a 2020 artifact. Community ordering is stable: c7 < c1 < c5 < c3 < c4 < c6 < c2 (most R to most D) in all three elections. Notable finding: c6 (Hispanic low-income) shows detectable realignment swing of +4.9pp→+1.2pp over election mean (2016→2020), consistent with documented FL Hispanic Republican shift. c6 and c4 (Asian) are negatively correlated across elections — the key structural signal for the Stan factor model's realignment factor. Historical election set is 2016+2018+2020; VEST series ends at 2021, no 2022 shapefile exists. | -- |
| 2026-03-18 | Census tract as primary unit of analysis (9,393 tracts in FL+GA+AL per 2022 ACS; early estimate of ~4,200 was wrong), replacing the original county assumption. | Counties suffer from the MAUP problem — heterogeneous places get averaged into single units. Blocks lack ACS data. Tracts are designed to approximate socially homogeneous neighborhoods, ACS is available at tract level, and VEST election data aggregates from block level. Transitional tracts provide built-in soft-assignment validation. | [ADR-004](adr/004-census-tract-unit-of-analysis.md) |
| 2026-03-10 | Hybrid Python + R + Stan stack: Python for data assembly and community detection, R for MRP and Bayesian modeling, Stan models as shared artifacts callable from both. | Best tool per task. Community detection libraries (leidenalg, graph-tool, scikit-learn NMF) are Python. The closest existing election model (Economist 2020) and the best MRP pipeline (ccesMRPprep) are R. Stan is the natural choice for custom Bayesian models and is callable from both ecosystems via CmdStanPy/CmdStanR. Forcing everything into one language would mean using weaker tools for at least one stage. | [ADR-001](adr/001-hybrid-python-r-stan.md) |
| 2026-03-10 | Community types as soft assignments: each county is a mixture of types (topic model / NMF framing) rather than belonging to exactly one type. | Counties are heterogeneous. A county with 40% evangelical rural, 30% military retiree, and 30% suburban professional population should not be forced into a single category. Soft assignment enables spectral unmixing for poll decomposition and produces more accurate county-level predictions for heterogeneous places. The NMF / topic-model analogy is well-established (counties as "documents," community types as "topics," demographic/network features as "words"). | [ADR-002](adr/002-community-type-soft-assignment.md) |
| 2026-03-10 | FL+GA+AL as proof-of-concept geography (226 counties). | This region provides community diversity (Cuban, Puerto Rican, retiree, military, evangelical, Black Belt, suburban, university), political heterogeneity (Florida and Georgia have recent flip history, Alabama provides a safe-state anchor), cross-border community testing (North FL / South GA / South AL share community structure across state lines), and good data availability (Florida voter files are among the most accessible nationally). | [ADR-003](adr/003-fl-ga-al-proof-of-concept.md) |
| 2026-03-10 | Dual-output model: jointly model vote share and turnout at the community-type level. | Enables decomposition of election-to-election shifts into persuasion (vote share change at constant turnout), mobilization (turnout change at constant vote share), and composition (population/registration change). This decomposition is a key interpretive output of the model. Joint modeling also respects the empirical correlation between turnout and vote share within community types (Assumption A007). | -- |
| 2026-03-10 | Two-stage separation: discover community types from non-political data first, then validate political relevance second. | Prevents circularity. If community types are defined by political outcomes, the model's "prediction" is partially tautological. Stage 1 uses demographics, religion, commuting, migration, and social network data only. Stage 2 checks whether the discovered types exhibit meaningful political covariance. This separation is also a falsifiability criterion: if non-political community structure does not predict political covariance, the core thesis is wrong. | -- |
| 2026-03-10 | Use the Economist 2020 presidential model as the primary architectural template for the propagation stage. | The Economist model (Linzer, Lauderdale, and Silver -- open source, Stan-based) is the closest existing implementation to what the propagation stage needs: poll aggregation, state-level correlation structure, prior specification from fundamentals. The key extension is replacing their demographic correlation with community-type covariance. Starting from a working model reduces risk compared to building from scratch. | -- |
| 2026-03-10 | Factor-structured covariance (low-rank factor model) rather than full covariance matrix for community types. | With K community types and only T election cycles (where K >> T), a full K x K covariance matrix is unidentifiable. A factor model with F << K factors (Assumption A006 suggests F = 3-5) reduces the parameter count from O(K^2) to O(K * F) and regularizes the covariance estimate. This is standard practice in financial portfolio models with similar dimensionality constraints. | -- |
| 2026-03-10 | Voter stability as a strong prior in the Bayesian model. | Supported by Gelman et al. 2016 ("The Mythical Swing Voter"), Kalla & Broockman 2018 (minimal persuasion effects), and ANES panel data showing high individual vote stability. The prior encodes the belief that large type-level swings are unlikely and that most apparent movement comes from differential turnout and composition change. This prior can be relaxed if evidence warrants (see Assumption A001). | -- |
| 2026-03-18 | Tikhonov ridge regression for 2022/2024 community back-calculation. | Bayesian approach (bayesian_poll_update with Stan Sigma) fails for county-level back-calc: data precision (~670K per county × 67 counties) dominates prior precision (~500) by 1340:1 — posterior exits [0,1]. Vote-normalized Tikhonov solves `min_{0≤θ≤1} \|\|W_w·θ - y_w\|\|² + λ\|\|θ - θ₀\|\|²`. Adaptive λ search finds minimum regularization keeping estimates physical. Prior chain: Stan(2016-2020) → 2022 governor → 2024 president. AL excluded from 2022 (MEDSL data quality); falls back to 2020 prior. | -- |
| 2026-03-18 | Stage 4 poll propagation implemented in Python (not R+Stan MRP). | Lightweight Gaussian Bayesian update in `src/propagation/propagate_polls.py`. Uses Stan covariance matrix but does not require running Stan for each forecast. Full MRP (R+Stan) deferred to post-MVP; Python approach sufficient for state-level polls. | -- |
| 2026-03-18 | 2026 forward prediction pipeline complete (placeholder polls). | FL Senate: polls 43.9% → model 39.5% (R+21); GA Senate: polls 50.5% → model 43.4% (R+13). Model applies structural downward correction to FL consistent with 2022/2024 validation showing ~5pp poll overestimate of Democrats. | -- |
| 2026-03-18 | RCMS 2020 religious data integrated (county level). | `fetch_rcms.py` scrapes ARDA county map tool. 6 derived features → `data/assembled/county_rcms_features.parquet`. RCMS is county-level only; ACS tract features remain separate. | -- |
| 2026-03-18 | IRS SOI county-to-county migration edge list integrated. | Downloads IRS SOI inflow CSVs (3 latest year pairs). Inflow-only design avoids double-counting. Feature computation from edge list deferred to post-MVP. | -- |
| 2026-03-19 | County level is the primary model engine; tracts are a future refinement layer. | Tract-level clustering produces r=-0.14 on holdout (data artifact: 6/9 shift dims are county-level MEDSL, so all tracts within a county receive identical values). County-level clustering produces r=0.93–0.98. County is the realistic common denominator for 2000–2024 data. | -- |
| 2026-03-19 | County model extended to 2000–2024 via MEDSL presidential + Algara/Amlani governor. | 3-cycle baseline extended to 12 cycles. Training dims: 6 → 30. Sources: MEDSL doi:10.7910/DVN/VOQCHQ; Algara/Amlani doi:10.7910/DVN/DGUMFI. AL 2018 governor was contested (Ivey vs Maddox, ~60/40) — real data used. | -- |
| 2026-03-19 | Multi-year holdout r=0.87 vs 3-cycle baseline r=0.98 (delta ~-0.11). | Multi-year model is slightly weaker on short-run holdout: 30 dims average over 20 years of history, pulling communities toward older patterns. Temporal depth and structural robustness are the multi-year model's value, not short-run autocorrelation. | -- |
| 2026-03-19 | Phase 1 K selection: K=10 (holdout r=0.9027). | K selection sweep over K=5..30 with spatial Ward HAC and min community size 8. K=10 maximizes holdout Pearson r between community-mean training shifts and holdout shifts. | -- |
| 2026-03-19 | Phase 2 API architecture: FastAPI + DuckDB read-only + in-process Bayesian update. | FastAPI opens wethervane.duckdb read-only at startup; loads K×K sigma, mu_prior, and weight matrices into app.state. Test isolation via create_app(lifespan_override=_noop_lifespan) factory + in-memory DuckDB fixture. | -- |
| 2026-03-19 | Phase 2 frontend: Next.js App Router + Deck.gl + Observable Plot. | Persistent choropleth map (left) + tabbed right panel. Visual style: Clean Academic (light background, Georgia serif headings, #2166ac/#d73027 partisan colors). | -- |
| 2026-03-20 | Type-primary architecture pivot (ADR-006). | Types become the primary predictive engine, replacing HAC geographic communities. KMeans on county shift vectors discovers J=43 types. HAC K=10 produced "alternative states" (10 giant blobs), not the stained glass pattern of many small units colored by behavioral type. | [ADR-006](adr/006-type-primary-architecture.md) |
| 2026-03-20 | KMeans for type discovery (not SVD/NMF). | SVD+varimax produced degenerate 2-type solution (r=0.35). NMF requires non-negative input. KMeans achieves holdout r=0.778 with presidential×2.5 weighting. | [ADR-006](adr/006-type-primary-architecture.md) |
| 2026-03-20 | Economist-inspired covariance construction (not Stan estimation). | Construct J×J type covariance from demographic profiles (Pearson correlation + shrinkage toward all-1s + PD enforcement). Deterministic Python, no sampling needed. Hybrid fallback if off-diagonal r < 0.4. | -- |
| 2026-03-20 | Hierarchical type nesting: J fine types → 5-8 super-types. | Fine types give model resolution; super-types give public interpretability. Ward HAC on type loadings (no spatial constraint). Super-types are the "colors" of the stained glass map. | -- |
| 2026-03-22 | J=20→43 based on leave-one-pair-out CV sweep. | Formal CV across J=12..50. Optimal: J=43 (r=0.779 CV mean). Performance gain: holdout r 0.778→0.818 (+4%). | -- |
| 2026-04-07 | Governor-specific Ridge priors for governor race forecasts. | Presidential Ridge priors (trained on 2024 pres outcomes) are structurally wrong for governor races — different electorate, turnout, and swing patterns. Governor Ridge (trained on Algara 2006-2018 + MEDSL 2022 governor data) achieves holdout r=0.696 vs 2022 actuals (mean baseline r=0.594). Lower r than presidential baseline (0.800) but half the bias (+2.2pp vs +4.6pp). The bias reduction is the primary value — direction + calibration matter more than raw correlation for live forecasts. | -- |
| 2026-04-07 | Incumbency heuristic as interim governor adjustment (+4pp toward incumbent party). | 10/36 governor predictions were WRONG direction (IL lean_r most egregious — should be safe_d). Simple +4pp for non-open-seat races fixes 3, improves 4. Clearly documented as interim until behavior layer ships. Removable constant in `_helpers.py`. | -- |
| 2026-04-07 | Behavior layer (τ-reweighted δ) implemented but DISABLED. | Three-step algorithm: θ_j type means → τ reweights expected off-cycle shares → residual δ captures genuine choice shift. δ std dropped 4% with τ reweighting (τ absorbs variance). However, model without δ achieves r=0.801 vs with δ r=0.790 against 2022 actuals. The behavior layer improves calibration (lower RMSE) at cost of correlation. Disabled until more governor data or governor-specific δ training is available. | -- |
| 2026-04-07 | Governor holdout validates temporal generalization (train ≤2018, predict 2022). | True temporal holdout: r=0.696, beats naive mean baseline (r=0.594). Presidential baseline r=0.800 but 2x bias. Biggest outliers are candidate effects (WY, MA, MD) — structurally unpredictable from demographics alone. Governor priors are NOT overfit despite single-cycle target. | -- |
| 2026-04-07 | Blended governor priors (70/30 governor/presidential) adopted for production. | Temporal holdout sweep across 11 blend weights. gov_weight=0.7 achieves r=0.816 with bias=+2.96pp — strictly dominates both pure baselines. Composite metric (r - |bias|/10) peaks at 0.7. Pure governor peaks in correlation at w=0.4 (r=0.848) but with more bias (+3.67pp). Deployed to live site. | -- |
| 2026-04-08 | CES external validation confirms type model quality (r=0.894). | 248K validated voters from Harvard CES (2006-2022) mapped to 80/100 types with 99.9% county FIPS match. Type-level D-share from CES correlates r=0.894 with model type priors. +3.57pp systematic D bias consistent with YouGov panel lean. First external validation of type structure against independent survey data. | -- |
| 2026-04-08 | Behavior layer δ DISABLED — δ is cycle-specific noise, not a type property. | Definitive temporal stability analysis: governor δ has mean cross-year r=0.091 across 5 midterm cycles (2006-2022). Compare τ: mean cross-year r=0.599 (structural signal). CES δ backtest: r drops from 0.839 to 0.777. Model δ backtest: r drops from 0.839 to 0.793. δ varies by candidate/cycle, not by type. Route choice-level signal through polls and candidate effects instead. τ (turnout composition) retained. | -- |
