## Papers

| ID | Citation | URL | Relevance |
|----|----------|-----|-----------|
| P001 | Gelman, Goel, Rivers, Rothschild. 2016. "The Mythical Swing Voter." *Quarterly Journal of Political Science* 11(1): 103-130. | https://doi.org/10.1561/100.00015031 | Phantom swings framework. Establishes that much apparent persuasion in topline polls is differential nonresponse. Core motivation for the voter-stickiness assumption. |
| P002 | Kalla, Joshua L. and David E. Broockman. 2018. "The Minimal Persuasive Effects of Campaign Contact in General Elections: Evidence from 49 Field Experiments." *American Political Science Review* 112(1): 148-166. | https://doi.org/10.1017/S0003055417000363 | Empirical support for voter stickiness. Campaign contact has near-zero persuasion effects in general elections, implying that election-to-election swings arise more from mobilization and composition than from persuasion. |
| P003 | Grimmer, Justin, Eitan Hersh, Marc Meredith, Jonathan Mummolo, and Clayton Nall. 2021. "Not by Turnout Alone: Measuring the Sources of Electoral Change, 2012-2020." Working paper. | https://justingrimmer.org/papers/ | Turnout decomposition methodology. Decomposes election swings into persuasion, mobilization, and composition effects using voter file data. Direct methodological precedent for the dual-output (vote share + turnout) model. |
| P004 | Bailey, Michael, Rachel Cao, Theresa Kuchler, Johannes Stroebel, and Arlene Wong. 2018. "Social Connectedness: Measurement, Determinants, and Effects." *Journal of Economic Perspectives* 32(3): 259-280. | https://doi.org/10.1257/jep.32.3.259 | Facebook Social Connectedness Index. Demonstrates that social ties predict economic and political similarity across geographies. Potential edge-weight source for the community graph. |
| P005 | Linzer, Drew A. 2013. "Dynamic Bayesian Forecasting of Presidential Elections in the States." *Journal of the American Statistical Association* 108(501): 124-134. | https://doi.org/10.1080/01621459.2012.737735 | Dynamic Bayesian state-space model for election forecasting. Establishes the methodological baseline for poll aggregation with temporal dynamics. Key reference for the forecasting layer of this project. |
| P006 | Heidemanns, Merlin, Andrew Gelman, and G. Elliott Morris. 2020. "An Updated Dynamic Bayesian Forecasting Model for the US Presidential Election." *Harvard Data Science Review* 2(4). | https://doi.org/10.1162/99608f92.fc62f1e1 | The Economist 2020 presidential model. Combines state-level polls, fundamentals, and prior election results in a hierarchical Bayesian framework. Direct predecessor in the poll-aggregation space; this project extends the approach by replacing state-level structure with community-level structure. |
| P007 | Ghitza, Yair and Andrew Gelman. 2013. "Deep Interactions with MRP: Election Turnout and Voting Patterns Among Small Electoral Subgroups." *American Journal of Political Science* 57(3): 762-776. | https://doi.org/10.1111/ajps.12004 | MRP with deep demographic interactions. Demonstrates that fine-grained demographic interactions matter for subnational estimation. Methodological precedent for the structured-prior approach to small-area estimation. |
| P008 | Gao, Yuxiang, Lauren Kennedy, Daniel Simpson, and Andrew Gelman. 2021. "Improving Multilevel Regression and Poststratification with Structured Priors." *Bayesian Analysis* 16(3): 719-744. | https://doi.org/10.1214/20-BA1223 | Structured priors for MRP. Shows that encoding demographic structure (e.g., age adjacency, education ordering) into priors improves small-area estimation. Directly relevant to how community-type structure could be incorporated as a prior. |
| P009 | Erikson, Robert S. and Christopher Wlezien. 2012. *The Timeline of Presidential Elections: How Campaigns Do and Do Not Matter.* University of Chicago Press. | https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/T/bo13948250.html | Establishes the empirical timeline of when and how campaigns affect vote intention. Important context for understanding what the forecasting model should and should not expect polls to capture at different time horizons. |
| P010 | Sides, John and Lynn Vavreck. 2013. *The Gamble: Choice and Chance in the 2012 Presidential Election.* Princeton University Press. | https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691156880/the-gamble | Demonstrates the "minimal effects" paradigm applied to the 2012 election. Reinforces the voter-stickiness assumption and the importance of fundamentals over campaign events. |
| P011 | King, Gary. 1997. *A Solution to the Ecological Inference Problem: Reconstructing Individual Behavior from Aggregate Data.* Princeton University Press. | https://gking.harvard.edu/eicamera/kinroot.html | Ecological inference methodology. Foundational for estimating individual-level behavior (e.g., turnout by race) from aggregate (precinct or county) data. Directly relevant to decomposing county-level returns into community-type contributions. |
| P012 | Lazarsfeld, Paul F., Bernard Berelson, and Hazel Gaudet. 1948. *The People's Choice: How the Voter Makes Up His Mind in a Presidential Campaign.* Columbia University Press. | -- | Columbia model of voting. Establishes that social context (religion, class, neighborhood) determines political behavior more than individual rational deliberation. The intellectual origin of the "who you talk to daily" theory underlying this project. |
| P013 | Green, Donald P. and Bradley Palmquist. 1990. "Of Artifacts and Partisan Instability." *American Journal of Political Science* 34(3): 872-902. | https://doi.org/10.2307/2111401 | Partisan stability. Demonstrates that much apparent partisan instability in panel surveys is measurement error, not true attitude change. Supports the voter-stickiness assumption. |
| P014 | Mummolo, Jonathan and Clayton Nall. 2017. "Why Partisans Do Not Sort: The Constraints on Political Segregation." *Journal of Politics* 79(1): 45-59. | https://doi.org/10.1086/687569 | Constraints on partisan sorting. Shows that most Americans do not move for political reasons; residential sorting is limited by economic and family constraints. | P015 | Martin, Gregory J. and Steven W. Webster. 2020. "Does Residential Sorting Explain Geographic Polarization?" *Political Science Research and Methods* 8(2): 215-231. | https://doi.org/10.1017/psrm.2018.44 | Complements Mummolo and Nall. Relevant to understanding which community-level shifts are persuasion vs. composition. |
| P016 | Bishop, Bill. 2008. *The Big Sort: Why the Clustering of Like-Minded America Is Tearing Us Apart.* Houghton Mifflin. | -- | Popular account of geographic partisan clustering. | P017 | Enos, Ryan D. 2017. *The Space Between Us: Social Geography and Politics.* Cambridge University Press. | https://doi.org/10.1017/9781108354943 | Social geography and political behavior. | P018 | Huckfeldt, Robert and John Sprague. 1987. "Networks in Context: The Social Flow of Political Information." *American Political Science Review* 81(4): 1197-1216. | https://doi.org/10.2307/1962585 | Network context and political information flow. Establishes that political discussion networks are constrained by social context (neighborhood, workplace, church). Direct empirical foundation for the "who you talk to daily" operationalization. |
| P019 | Flaxman, Seth, Sharad Goel, and Justin M. Rao (with Alexander Smola). 2015. "Who Supported Obama in 2012? | P020 | Tikhonov, Gleb, Otso Ovaskainen, Jari Oksanen, Melinda de Jonge, Oystein Opedal, and Tad Dallas. 2020. "Joint Species Distribution Modelling with the R-package Hmsc." *Methods in Ecology and Evolution* 11(3): 442-447. | https://doi.org/10.1111/2041-210X.13345 | HMSC joint species distribution modeling. Methodological analogy for joint modeling of community-type vote shares. |
| P021 | Cai, Deng, Xiaofei He, Jiawei Han, and Thomas S. Huang. 2011. "Graph Regularized Nonnegative Matrix Factorization for Data Representation." *IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence* 33(8): 1548-1560. | https://doi.org/10.1109/TPAMI.2010.231 | Graph-regularized NMF. | P022 | Rasti, Behnood, et al. 2024. "HySUPP: Hyperspectral Unmixing Python Package." *arXiv preprint.* | https://github.com/BehnoodRasti/HySUPP | Spectral unmixing software and methods. Linear and nonlinear unmixing algorithms directly applicable to the topic-model / spectral-unmixing framing of counties as mixtures of community types. |
| P023 | Mayhew, David R. 2002. *Electoral Realignments: A Critique of an American Genre.* Yale University Press. | https://yalebooks.yale.edu/book/9780300093360/electoral-realignments/ | Critique of realignment theory. Argues that sharp, durable realignments are rarer than commonly claimed. Relevant to the temporal-stability assumption and to interpreting community-type shifts across election cycles. |
| P024 | Zingher, Joshua N. 2022. "The Diploma Divide: Educational Attainment and the Realignment of the American Electorate." *Political Research Quarterly* 75(2): 265-281. | https://doi.org/10.1177/10659129211019507 | Education realignment. | P025 | Pons, Vincent. 2018. "Will a Five-Minute Discussion Change Your Mind? Complements Kalla and Broockman (P002) with international evidence. Door-to-door canvassing affects turnout more than vote choice, reinforcing the mobilization emphasis. |

### Paper Notes

- Papers P001, P002, P003, P013 form the empirical backbone for the **voter-stickiness assumption**.
- Papers P005, P006, P009, P026 define the **poll aggregation and forecasting** methodological space.
- Papers P007, P008, P011, P019 address **small-area estimation and ecological inference**.
- Papers P012, P017, P018 establish the **social-context theory of political behavior**.
- Papers P014, P015, P016 inform the **residential sorting and community stability** questions.
- Papers P020, P021, P022, P030 provide **methodological analogies** from ecology, signal processing, and spatial ML.

## Methods

| ID | Method | Source Field | Key Packages | Relevance | Key Reference |
|----|--------|-------------|--------------|-----------|---------------|
| M001 | Graph-regularized NMF | Machine learning / signal processing | scikit-learn (NMF baseline), custom implementation with graph Laplacian penalty | Core method candidate for discovering community types. NMF decomposes the county-by-feature matrix into community-type profiles (W) and county mixing proportions (H). Graph regularization via the county adjacency/connectivity graph ensures that geographically or socially connected counties receive similar mixing proportions. | P021 (Cai et al. 2011) |
| M002 | Multi-layer Leiden community detection | Network science | leidenalg (Python), igraph | Detects community structure in the multi-layer county graph (layers: commuting, social connectivity, migration, religious similarity). Leiden improves on Louvain with guaranteed connectivity of detected communities. Multi-layer variant finds communities that are coherent across multiple relationship types simultaneously. | Traag, Waltman, van Eck 2019. "From Louvain to Leiden." *Scientific Reports.* |
| M003 | Nested stochastic block model | Statistical network science | graph-tool (Python) | Non-parametric Bayesian community detection that automatically determines the number of communities and their hierarchical nesting. Produces the hierarchical structure (types within blocs within mega-blocs) without requiring the analyst to specify the number of levels or groups. More principled than modularity-based methods for determining community count. | Peixoto 2014. "Hierarchical Block Structures and High-Resolution Model Selection in Large Networks." *Physical Review X.* |
| M004 | Infomap flow-based community detection | Information theory / network science | infomap (R023) | Detects communities by finding the partition that minimizes the description length of a random walk on the network. Particularly well-suited for directed and weighted flow networks (commuting flows, migration flows). Naturally handles hierarchical structure via the multi-level map equation. | Rosvall and Bergstrom 2008. "Maps of Random Walks on Complex Networks Reveal Community Structure." *PNAS.* |
| M005 | MRP with structured priors | Political science / survey statistics | Stan, brms, rstanarm | Multilevel Regression and Poststratification for estimating subnational opinion from national surveys. Structured priors encode known relationships between demographic categories (e.g., adjacent age groups should have similar effects). This project extends MRP by adding community-type as a grouping variable with structured priors reflecting the discovered community hierarchy. | P007 (Ghitza & Gelman 2013), P008 (Gao et al. 2021) |
| M006 | Linear spectral unmixing | Remote sensing / signal processing | HySUPP (R018), unmixing (R019), pysptools | Decomposes each county's observed political-demographic profile as a linear mixture of pure community-type endmembers. Assumes additivity: a county's behavior is the weighted sum of its component community types' behaviors. Constrained variants enforce non-negativity and sum-to-one on mixing proportions. | P022 (Rasti et al. 2024) |
| M007 | HHH4 endemic-epidemic model | Epidemiology / time series | surveillance (R package) | Time-series model originally developed for infectious disease counts. Decomposes counts into endemic (baseline/trend), epidemic (contagion from neighbors), and autoregressive components. Potentially applicable to modeling how political shifts propagate through the community network: endemic = national trend, epidemic = contagion from connected communities, AR = local persistence. | Held and Paul 2012. "Modeling Seasonality in Space-Time Infectious Disease Surveillance Data." *Biometrical Journal.* |
| M008 | HMSC joint species distribution model | Community ecology | Hmsc (R, R022), equivalent custom implementation in Python/Stan | Models multiple response variables (community-type vote shares or turnout rates) jointly as functions of environmental covariates and latent factors capturing residual covariance. The residual covariance matrix reveals which community types tend to shift together beyond what covariates explain. Hierarchical structure allows species-level (community-type) and site-level (county) random effects. | P020 (Tikhonov et al. 2020) |
| M009 | LightFM hybrid collaborative filtering | Recommender systems | lightfm (Python) | Hybrid collaborative filtering that combines content-based features (county demographics) with interaction data (election results). Can be framed as: counties are "users," elections are "items," vote shares are "ratings," and community-type features help predict unobserved county-election combinations. Useful for the poll-propagation step. | Kula 2015. "Metadata Embeddings for User and Item Cold-start Recommendations." |
| M010 | PyGSP graph signal interpolation | Graph signal processing | PyGSP (Python) | Treats vote shares and poll results as signals on the county connectivity graph. Graph signal processing tools (graph Fourier transform, spectral filtering, signal interpolation) can propagate observed poll values to unobserved counties while respecting graph structure. Smoothness assumption: counties connected in the community graph should have similar political signals. | Perraudin et al. 2014. "GSPBOX: A Toolbox for Signal Processing on Graphs." |
| M011 | Dynamic Bayesian state-space model | Statistics / time series | Stan, PyMC, statsmodels | Models the evolution of latent state (true vote intention) over time, with polls as noisy observations. State equation captures random walk or mean-reverting dynamics; observation equation models poll bias and variance. This project extends the standard approach (P005, P006) by making the state vector community-type-level rather than state-level. | P005 (Linzer 2013), P006 (Heidemanns et al. 2020) |
| M012 | Ensemble Kalman filter | Data assimilation / geoscience | filterpy (Python), custom implementation | Sequential data assimilation method that updates an ensemble of state estimates as new observations (polls) arrive. Computationally efficient alternative to full MCMC for the dynamic forecasting layer, especially when the state dimension (number of community types x time steps) is large. Used extensively in weather forecasting and oceanography for high-dimensional state estimation. | Evensen 2003. "The Ensemble Kalman Filter: Theoretical Formulation and Practical Implementation." *Ocean Dynamics.* |
| M013 | Variation partitioning | Community ecology / multivariate statistics | vegan (R), scikit-learn (Python adaptation) | Decomposes explained variance in community-type vote shares into unique and shared contributions of multiple predictor sets (e.g., demographics, geography, community structure, economic indicators). Directly addresses the explanatory ambition: how much variance does community structure explain beyond demographics and geography? Uses partial RDA or partial CCA. | Borcard, Legendre, and Drapeau 1992. "Partialling Out the Spatial Component of Ecological Variation." *Ecology.* |
| M014 | OAC geodemographic classification | Geography / marketing science | scikit-learn (k-means, hierarchical clustering), custom pipelines (R021) | Output Area Classification. Classifies small geographic areas into types based on demographic, socioeconomic, and housing characteristics. Standard methodology: standardize Census variables, apply dimensionality reduction (PCA), cluster (k-means or hierarchical). Provides a methodological template for the community-type discovery step, though this project extends it with graph regularization and political outcome data. | Vickers and Rees 2007. "Creating the UK National Statistics 2001 Output Area Classification." *JRSS-A.* |
| M015 | Gaussian belief propagation | Probabilistic graphical models | pgmpy (Python), custom implementation | Message-passing algorithm for inference in Gaussian graphical models. On the county connectivity graph, each county sends messages about its estimated political state to its neighbors, and updates its own estimate based on received messages. Converges to the exact posterior in trees and to good approximations in loopy graphs. Candidate for the poll-propagation step as an alternative to M010 or M012. | Bickson 2009. "Gaussian Belief Propagation: Theory and Application." PhD thesis, Hebrew University. |
